modern AI predominantly relies on deep learning and reinforcement learning algorithms, which have driven major breakthroughs in fields like computer vision and natural language processing. Some of the most famous and recently used 
algorithms include: 

### Deep Learning Algorithms:
Deep learning involves neural networks with multiple layers to process large amounts of data and learn complex patterns, which is inspired by the human brain. 

**Transformers**: This architecture uses a self-attention meachanism to weigh the significance
of different parts of the input data. This methd effectively handles the context over long sequences

**Appliaction**: Transformers are the basic for modern large language modls like OpeaAI's GPT series and Google's  
Gemini. They are used for text generatin, translation, and summarization

**Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)**: CNNs are designed to automatically and adaptively
 learn spatial hierarchies in data by applying filters to extract featuers.

 Application: CNNs are used for image and video processing tasks, including image 
 recognition, object detection and medical imagining analysis.
 **Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)**: This framework has two competing neural networks. A "generator" creates data, and a "discriminator" evaluates its authenticity.
 Application: GNNs generate realistic images, videos (deepfakes) and  synthetic data for tasks like data 
 augmentation.

 ** Diffusion Models**: These are a type of generative model that starts with random noise and gradually "denoises" it to produce a meaningful result.
 

Application: Diffusion Models power text-to-image generators like Stable Diffusion and DALL-E 2.

**Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs):Are specialized  for processing sequential data by using an internal memory.
Applications: Although largely superseded by Transformers in many NLP tasks, LSTMs (a variant of RNNs) are still used for time-series forecasting and sequence analysis. 


##Reinforcement learning algorithms
Reinforcement learning trains an agent to make a sequence of decisions in an environment 
to maximise a cumulative reward.
**Q-Learning**
: This is a model-free algorithm that learns a quality or "Q-value" for state-action pairs to guide an agent's behavior.

Application: Q-Learning is used in robotics and game-playing to achieve superhuman skill levels 

**Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO)**: PPO is a policy-based algorinm that trais agents by providing a policy network with a set 
of actions that results in the desired outcomes.

Appications: PPO is used to beat professional players in complex games like Dota2


**Deep Q-Network (DQN)**: DQN combines Q-learning with deep neural networks , allowing it to 
handle complex, high-dimensional inupts like raw pixel data.

Application: DQN is known for achieving superhuman performance in playing classic Atari video games.

##Ensemble and traditional algorithms
Not all famous and modern algorithms  are deep learning-based. Loder tranditional algorithsm 
are often combined to create powerful ensemble models.

**Gradient Boosting (eg: XBoost, LightGBM, CatBoost)**: This builds a strong predictive model
by sequentially combining multiple "weak" models, often decision trees.

Applications: Gradient Boosting is top choice for winning machine learning competitions.
It is a powerful tool for structured data tasks like predictive analyitics.

**Random Forest**: This is an ensemble method that  constructs multiple decision trees during training 
and outputs the mode (for classification) or mean (for regression)  of the individual trees.

Applications: Random Forest is known for its ability to reduce overfitting and handle large datasets effectively.
